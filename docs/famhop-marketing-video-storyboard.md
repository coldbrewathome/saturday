# FamHop Marketing Video Storyboard

## Primary Creative Brief

Duration: 30 seconds

Primary format: 9:16 vertical for TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and mobile ads.

Secondary crops: Keep the phone mockup, parent, and key copy inside the center 70% so the same video can be cropped to 1:1 or 16:9.

Audience: Parents, caregivers, and co-parents planning a kid-friendly weekend.

Core promise: FamHop turns "what should we do this weekend?" into a shareable three-stop family plan.

Tone: Warm, practical, modern, lightly playful. Avoid cartoon chaos, guilt, or overly polished luxury-parent imagery.

Visual style: Real families, phone UI closeups, map pins, neighborhood movement, warm daylight, clean app-like overlays. The product should feel fast and useful, not like a generic family montage.

CTA: Plan your weekend at famhop.com

## One-Line Concept

Weekend planning starts as a messy group chat, then FamHop turns it into a simple kid-ready plan everyone can vote on.

## Master Prompt For Video Generation

Create a polished 30-second mobile-first marketing video for FamHop, a family weekend planning website. The video follows a parent trying to plan Saturday with kids. Start with a messy group chat and indecision, then show a clean mobile website experience where the parent chooses kids' ages, interests, constraints, and a vibe. FamHop instantly creates a three-stop family plan with kid-friendly places like parks, libraries, museums, food stops, and local events. The parent shares the plan with another caregiver, who votes on the options. End with the FamHop logo and the line "Plan the weekend together."

Use warm natural light, real city-neighborhood scenes, friendly but realistic family moments, clean mobile UI overlays, map pins, plan cards, and share/vote interactions. Make the product feel fast, practical, and trustworthy. Do not use cartoon characters, fantasy visuals, stock-photo cliches, or exaggerated chaos. Keep all key UI and text centered for vertical social video. Use simple on-screen typography and leave room for captions.
